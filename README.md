Pharmaceutical Retail Revenue Optimization: Integrating Market Basket Analysis and Product Profitability.

A large pharmaceutical retail organization operating multiple pharmacy outlets across India generates millions of sales transactions each year. These outlets cater to diverse customer segments, resulting in vast volumes of transactional, product, and revenue data.

Despite having access to extensive sales information, management often struggles to transform this data into actionable business insights. Decision-making is frequently driven by overall sales figures rather than a deeper understanding of product profitability, regional purchasing trends, and customer buying behavior.

Consequently, products with high sales volumes are not always the most profitable, while highly profitable medicines may receive limited attention. Furthermore, the organization often lacks visibility into products that are frequently purchased together, leading to missed opportunities for cross-selling, product bundling, and targeted promotional strategies.

To address these challenges, there is a need for a data-driven approach that integrates market basket analysis with product profitability assessment, enabling the organization to optimize revenue, improve product placement strategies, and support informed business decision-making across its retail network.

Business Problems-

1.Identifying High-Sales but Low-Profit Medicines
2.Lack of Knowledge About Products Purchased Together
3.Missed Cross-Selling Opportunities
4.Inventory Investment in Low-Performing Medicines
5.Increasing Average Transaction Value

Research Objectives
To analyze the profitability of pharmaceutical products using sales and pricing data.
To identify medicines frequently purchased together through market basket analysis.
To discover cross-selling opportunities that can increase transaction value.
To develop an interactive Excel dashboard that supports data-driven decision-making for retail pharmacies.
To provide actionable recommendations for revenue optimization, inventory planning, and product placement strategies
